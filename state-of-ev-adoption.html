Canada's EV Roadblock: Barriers to Adoption

Uncovering the challenges facing EV adoption in Canada through a multi-format, comprehensive analysis.

Myah Riddell

November 2024

Around the world, progressive nations have acknowledged the importance of decarbonizing the transportation sector, a major force behind rising greenhouse gas emissions. Electric vehicles offer a modern, climate-conscious catalyst to reimagine personal transportation. In 2023, the Canadian federal government introduced ambitious mandates requiring all new light-duty vehicles sold in the country to be zero-emission by 2035. Despite these measures, Canadians remain reluctant to embrace the future.

Understanding this reluctance requires examining the federal government’s actions—and inactions—alongside infrastructure, equity, and affordability challenges that threaten Canada’s dream of an electrified future.
